Mama's Buttermilk Cornbread

Out of all the many ways cornbread can be prepared, very little come close to your own mother's homemade buttermilk cornbread. With our recipe, you'll be able to make a a whole cornbread just how your mother would, matching the nostalgic taste, goodness, and comfort she put into each dish she prepared for you.

Before we get too cookin', make sure you have all the necessary tools to make a full loaf of cornbread, including the pot or pan of your desire, meeasuring cups, and measuring spoons. Our recipe will provide nine servings, so make sure to take that into account when choosing a cooking pan. The ingredients we will need are butter, white sugar, eggs, buttermilk, baking soda, cornmeal, all-purpose flour, and salt (measurments are provided in the ingredients list below).

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup butter
  • 2/3 cup white sugar
  • 1 cup buttermilk
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 cup cornmeal
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt

Directions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375 degrees F (175 degrees C). Grease your square pan (should be about 8 inches).
  2. Next, melt some butter in a large skillet over medium heat. Remove from the heat after melted and stir in sugar. Quickly mix in eggs.
  3. Combine buttermilk and baking soda into a small bown , then whisk the mixture into the skillet. Stir in cornmeal, flour, and salt until mixture is well-blended and contains very little lumps. Pour your batter into the prepared pan.
  4. Bake in your preheated oven until a toothpick inserted in the center of the batch comes out clean (30 to 40 minutes).

Congratulations! You have sucessfully made your very own homemade buttermilk cornbread! Now grab your friends and relatives and help yourselves to a nostalgic, warm, and comforting serving of Mama's Buttermilk Cornbread!
